Volunteer and Emergency Service Experience

First Responder, Event Health Services | St John Ambulance Australia

July 2025 – Present

Expand ▼

In my role with St John Ambulance, I provide medical support and advanced first aid at a range of events, from large, high-pressure gatherings to smaller local activities. My focus is always on achieving the best outcomes for patients. This means I take care to assess each situation thoroughly, work closely with the Queensland Ambulance Service during handovers, and make sure my communication is clear and reliable throughout each operation.

So far, I have completed 118 hours across 16 events, both large and small.

Role Overview

  • Clinical Care & Scope Management: When providing clinical care, I make sure to follow Clinical Practice Guidelines and stay within my scope of practice. I recognise when a patient needs care beyond what I can provide, and I escalate to healthcare professionals when necessary.
  • Acute Injury Management: I have experience assessing and treating a wide range of injuries and medical emergencies, from minor trauma to more complex musculoskeletal injuries like fractures and dislocations.
  • Inter-agency Liaison: When caring for high-acuity patients, I make sure clinical handovers to the Queensland Ambulance Service are smooth and thorough. I do this by providing clear verbal and written reports to support continuity of care.
  • Operational Communications: I use radio communication to maintain situational awareness, provide concise SITREPs (situation reports), and coordinate resources with the Communications Centre.

Why I Joined

Joining St John Ambulance was a real turning point for me. Before joining St John Ambulance, I found communication difficult, which made everyday tasks feel challenging. Joining St John Ambulance gave me a way to ease into high-pressure environments at my own pace, and it was flexible with my needs. Because I could choose my shifts, I began with smaller events, such as school fairs, where I could focus more on building my patient communication skills. As I became more confident, I started taking on busier events, such as Oz-Tag and 3P-tag events, where I had to manage larger patient numbers and more complex injuries.

Furthermore, while my role at St John Ambulance only includes Advanced First Aid, I've still had the chance to gain insights into what a role in health care is like by working alongside registered nurses and paramedics. I've learned a lot about this line of work, which has further motivated me to turn it into a career, rather than a volunteer commitment.

Taking the time to get to know the patient and understand their experiences can make a significant difference in their outcome. Incident Reflection App

Personal Development Project

Expand ▼

I designed a web-based tool to help with self-reflection and professional growth in community service and first-response roles. By providing a structured way to record what went well and lessons learned during shifts, this app helps bridge the gap between field experience and clinical improvement.

App Overview

  • Reflective Practice: The app supports the Gibbs model of reflection by allowing me to document my own performance and peers' observations for professional growth.
  • Privacy-First Design: I designed the app to allow comprehensive reflection notes on the go, while ensuring no patient-identifiable information is recorded.
  • Agile Iteration: I developed it as a lightweight tool to practise state management and user-centred design in a fast-paced service context.

Purpose

Given my past communication struggles, I would write a paragraph at the end of each day about what went wrong and where I can improve my communication. This later evolved into me writing comprehensive Gibbs reflections in a PDF Portfolio after each St John shift, listing what I did well, and where I can improve. Now, whenever I start a new shift with St John Ambulance, I have a list of new communication skills recorded down, ready for me to practice. This reflection app helps me add to my portfolio by allowing me to record how I feel in the moment, what I think I did well, and what I want to improve. Recording this in the moment allows me to make a more accurate reflection later at home, as I've removed the need to remember exactly how I felt and every detail of the interaction.

Vehicle Pre-Check Application

Personal Development Project

Expand ▼

In this project, I set out to modernise and simplify the process of vehicle pre-start safety checks by building a streamlined digital utility. My aim was to see how digital checklists could help reduce human error and support operational readiness, especially in situations where equipment reliability really matters.

App Overview

  • Workflow Optimisation: One of my main goals was to optimise the workflow for routine safety checks. I did this by designing an interface that is both intuitive and mobile-friendly, which helps reduce the risk of missing something important.
  • Safety Compliance: I also wanted the app to closely follow standard operating procedures. By doing this, I could highlight the importance of thorough vehicle maintenance and make sure that pre-start accountability is built into the process.
  • Technical Practice: From a technical perspective, this project gave me a chance to practise building responsive form logic and using conditional rendering in front-end development.

Purpose

When I drive, I know it is my responsibility to make sure I am driving safely. One of the most important steps is to carry out a thorough pre-check before I start the car. If something like the engine coolant is low or the brakes are not working as they should, it can lead to serious damage or even put others at risk. That is why, unless I have checked the car myself or have watched someone else do it, I always assume the car has not been cleared yet. It is up to me to confirm that the car is ready to drive.

Since I regularly switch between different cars, like the Domino's and St John company vehicles, I make sure to do a full check each time. To help me avoid missing anything important, I have put together a comprehensive checklist that I follow whenever I check a car.
