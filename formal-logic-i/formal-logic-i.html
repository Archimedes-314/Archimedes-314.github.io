Temp Headding

In everyday conversations, the meaning of what we say often depends on context and tone. For example, the question "Why are you here?" can mean different things based on which word is emphasized. If I put stress on "here," as in, "Why are you here?", I'm asking why you chose this website instead of another one. If I put stress on "you", as in, "Why are you here!?", I'm asking why you, specifically, are here, which might suggest that others are welcome, but you are not.

In math, we avoid this kind of uncertainty. We have to explain mathematical results clearly so everyone understands them. That's why we use mathematical logic. Each symbol has just one meaning, and every statement follows strict rules, so everything stays clear.

In the following lessons, I'll show you how to turn everyday language into mathematical statements and explain what the different symbols mean.
